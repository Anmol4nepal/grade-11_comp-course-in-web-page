CHAPTER-1
COMPUTER SYSTEM

Introduction to Computers

Computer is an electronic device device derived from the Latin word "computere" which means to calculate. It accepts raw facts and figures as an input which are isolated and uninterpreted through input device, process it according to the requirement of the user or command supplied by the user, store it before and after processing of require and produce a meaningful information as an output through an output device. Generally, computer works on IPO (Input-Process-Output) cycle. It follows GIGO (Garbage In Garbage Out) algorithm which means the output is always based on the input supplied by the user.

Some Characteristics/Features of Computer

The implementation of computers is not only limited/confined to a small area, rather they are used from simple domestic tasks to complex scientific research and engineering. The use of computers is increasing day by day due to some special features or characteristics that computers possess. Several features of computers are:

  • Accuracy: Computers are the accurate machines, meaning the results produced by computers are 100% accurate. Since they follow GIGO, the error that may arise in output is due to human error, not the computer.
  • Speed: Computers work at tremendously high speed. The operating speed of computers is measured in milliseconds, microseconds, picoseconds, and nanoseconds, which means computers can perform millions and billions of instructions per second.
  • Storage: Storage is the area or unit capable of storing data and information for present and future use. Storing data allows users to retrieve it whenever required. Today, computers come with high volumes of memory measured in Megabytes (1024 KB), Gigabytes (1024 MB), Terabytes (1024 GB), and Petabytes (1024 TB).
  • Versatility: Computers are generally designed to serve more than one type of work. The implementation of computers is not only limited to specific purposes; they solve general user requirements and can be used in more than one type of job.
  • Diligence: The feature of computers in which they don't get tired even after working for long periods. Computers can perform any number of tasks continuously until accomplished. Once the instruction is given, the computer follows the command until it is terminated.
  • Automatic: A computer is an automatic machine. Once the instruction is generated, it performs accordingly until the command is terminated. We don't have to issue continuous commands to operate the computer. This is called automation.

Applications/Implementations/Uses of Computer

The uses of computers are increasing day by day. Every aspect of human life is highly influenced by computers. From simple domestic use to complex engineering and scientific solutions, computers are applied in numerous fields. This is due to the versatility of computers. Some of the basic implementations of computers are:

  • Education: Computers are highly used in the teaching and learning process. Many interesting graphics can be created and provided for easy learning. Additionally, computers provide internet access, making learning easy and fun.
  • Communication: Computers are massively used in communication, such as email, chat, online conferencing, and e-fax, all with the help of the internet. They have made communication faster, easier, and cheaper.
  • Business: In the business sector, computers are used to generate invoices, maintain stock, and make statistical analyses. E-commerce is a new and emerging way of buying and selling goods and services through electronic means and media.
  • Engineering and Designing: Scientific and engineering designs require complex and massive computations. The design of bridges, towers, buildings, generators, motors, electrical transmission, etc., requires precise computations that are done through computers.
  • Science and Research: Computers are essential for scientific research, where massive amounts of data need to be processed and analyzed.
  • Military: Computers play a critical role in the military, from simulations and strategic planning to communication and weaponry systems.
  • Industry: Computers are used in industrial applications for automation, inventory management, and process control.
  • Medicine: In the medical field, computers assist in diagnosing diseases, maintaining patient records, and performing complex medical simulations.
  • Robotics: Computers are used in robotics for controlling robotic arms, autonomous vehicles, and more.
  • Transportation: Computers are essential in managing and optimizing transportation systems, including air traffic control, traffic lights, and vehicle navigation systems.

Generations of Computers

The history of computers dates back to the mechanical era, progressing through various phases from mechanical to electro-mechanical to digital computers. The development of electronic components, precise engineering, and advanced technology has drastically changed computer operations. Below are the five generations of computers:

1. First Generation (1946-1958)

  • Used vacuum tubes for controlling and amplifying electronic signals.
  • Large size, high electricity consumption, and heat generation.
  • Prone to hardware failure and not used commercially.
  • Programmed using machine-level language.
  • Examples: ENIAC, EDVAC, EDSAC, UNIVAC.

2. Second Generation (1959-1964)

  • Used transistors as electronic components.
  • Smaller size, less electricity consumption, and reduced heat generation.
  • More reliable, though still not commercially viable due to high cost.
  • Programmed using assembly language.
  • Examples: IBM 7000, IBM 1401, IBM 1620, MARK III.

3. Third Generation (1965-1974)

  • Used Integrated Circuits (ICs).
  • Smaller, faster, and more reliable with less heat generation.
  • Widely used for commercial applications.
  • Programmed using high-level languages.
  • Examples: PDP-8, IBM 360, ICL 2900 series.

4. Fourth Generation (1975-1990)

  • Used Very Large Scale Integrated Circuits (VLSI).
  • Compact size, portable, faster processing, and more reliable.
  • Larger memory (GB and TB) and advanced high-level languages for programming.
  • Examples: Apple Macintosh, IBM PC.

5. Fifth Generation (1990 onwards)

  • Used Ultra Large Scale Integrated Circuits (ULSIC) or Microprocessors.
  • Incorporated artificial intelligence (AI) and parallel processing for high performance.
  • Capable of image processing, speech recognition, and other advanced features.
  • Programmed using various high-level languages.

Introduction to Computer System

A computer system is a combination of hardware, software, and peripherals integrated to work together. It follows the IPO (Input, Process, Output) cycle, converting raw data into meaningful information.

Components of Computer System

  • Hardware: Physical devices like CPU, input/output devices, storage devices.
  • Software: Programs that instruct hardware to perform tasks (e.g., Operating System, MS-Office).
  • Data/Information: Raw facts (data) processed into meaningful information.
  • People/User: Operate the system and generate commands.
  • Procedure: Describes how tasks are performed to convert data into information.
  • Communication: The flow of data between components of the system.

Basic Hardware Components

  • Input Unit: Accepts raw data and instructions from the user (e.g., keyboard, mouse).
  • Output Unit: Converts processed information into a form understandable by humans (e.g., monitor, printer).
  • Processing Unit: Performs computations and operations on input data (CPU).
  • Storage Unit: Stores data and instructions (e.g., RAM, hard disk).

Types of Output Devices

  • Hard Copy Output: Tangible outputs like printed documents (e.g., printer, plotter).
  • Soft Copy Output: Intangible outputs shown on a screen or through speakers (e.g., monitor, speakers).

Classification of Computers Based on Size

1. Super Computer

Supercomputers are the fastest and most powerful computers. They are used for specialized tasks like weather forecasting, rocket launching, and complex simulations. Due to their high cost and size, they are not commercially successful and require temperature-controlled rooms.

2. Mainframe Computer

Mainframe computers are large, fast, and expensive but not as powerful as supercomputers. They are used by large corporations and government agencies for tasks like census data processing. The first computer in Nepal, IBM 1401, was a mainframe computer used for population counting.

3. Mini Computer

Mini computers are smaller and less expensive than mainframes but more powerful than microcomputers. They are used in industries, universities, and banks for medium-sized business tasks. Examples include Data General and MAGNUM.

4. Micro Computer

Microcomputers are the smallest and least expensive computers. They use microprocessors and are commonly used as personal computers. They come in various forms:

  • Desktop: Personal computers that sit on a desk, with a range of processing speeds and storage capacities.
  • Laptop: Portable computers that can be carried around, popular among travelers and businesspeople. They run on battery and are highly portable.
  • Palmtop/Handheld: Small, highly portable devices, such as smartphones and PDAs, that fit in the palm of the hand and offer applications like gaming and internet browsing.
